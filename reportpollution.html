How to Report Pollution and Protect Our Waterways

Understanding Pollution and Its Impact:

Everyone contributes to pollution, which affects our streams, bays, and coastal waters. Wastewater, dirt, and debris from our daily activities eventually make their way into water systems. To keep our waterways clean and enjoyable, we need to reduce our pollution. Even natural materials like plant waste can cause problems like algal blooms and fish kills if they add excess nitrogen to water.

Brief Overview of the Form and Technologies Used

1. Purpose of the Form

The form is designed to allow users to report water quality issues or pollution incidents. It collects detailed information about the incident, including the location, time, and nature of the issue, and allows users to upload a photo. The form integrates with a map to help users pinpoint the location of the incident accurately.

2. Technologies and Components

  • JavaScript:
    • Date and Time Initialization: Sets the current date and time in the form fields when the page loads.
    • Leaflet.js:
      • Map Integration: Displays an interactive map allowing users to click on the map to set the location or zoom to their current location.
      • Geolocation: Uses browser geolocation to get the user's current position and update the map and form fields accordingly.
      • Reverse Geocoding: Converts latitude and longitude coordinates to a human-readable address using the Nominatim API.
      • Event Handling: Manages user interactions with the map, including setting markers and updating address information.
    • Form Handling:
      • Submission: Validates the form and sends data to the server using the Fetch API.
  • Leaflet.js: Enables integration of interactive maps on the webpage.
  • Nominatim API: Provides geocoding and reverse geocoding to accurately pinpoint the location of reported incidents.

3. Form Usage

  • User Interaction:
    • Map Integration: Click on the map to select the incident location and fetch the address.
    • Geolocation Button: Center the map on your current location and place a marker.
    • Manual Address Entry: Enter the address manually and validate it using a provided button.
  • Form Submission:
    • Validation: Ensures all fields are filled and location is confirmed.
    • Submission: Sends data to the server for processing.
  • Error Handling: Displays error messages for address or submission issues.

How to Report Pollution:

To report an incident, use the "Report an Incident" link and provide detailed information:

  • Describe the Incident:

    Give a thorough account of what you saw, including the exact location (address), the amount of material involved, and any related factors.

  • Provide Details:

    Mention if the weather was a factor, the time and duration of the incident, and any other relevant details.

  • Data Collection and Follow-Up:

    The investigator might call you for more information and will inform you about how the issue was resolved, provided the report is not anonymous.

By following these guidelines, you help keep our waterways clean and healthy.
