Overview

The Geocoding Water Quality Application is designed to revolutionize water quality monitoring by integrating advanced geocoding techniques with MongoDB. Our application enables effective location mapping and visualization of water quality data, leveraging a dynamic system to enhance environmental insights and decision-making.

Key Components:

  • Geocoding: Convert user-provided addresses into geographic coordinates for map display.
  • Clustered Display: Visualize water quality data with clustering techniques to group nearby data points for clearer insights.
  • MongoDB Integration: Store and manage water quality and location data efficiently, supporting quick access and spatial queries.
  • Location Mapping: Map water quality data based on geocoded addresses, providing a clear visual representation of data distribution.
  • Nominating Method: Allow users to nominate and input addresses, which are geocoded and displayed on the map for analysis.
1. Water Quality Form
Purpose: Our Water Quality Form allows users to input detailed water quality data, which is then processed and stored in MongoDB. This data is crucial for building a comprehensive water quality index that is visualized on a geocoding map.
Features:
  • Detailed Input Fields: Enter information such as location, pH levels, TDS (Total Dissolved Solids), turbidity, and more.
  • Dynamic Dropdowns: Dropdowns are populated dynamically from our database, ensuring up-to-date options.
  • Custom Input Option: If the predefined options don’t cover your needs, you can enter custom values.
How to Use:
  • Access the Form: Navigate to the Water Quality Form through the link below.
  • Fill in Data: Provide detailed information about water quality and location.
  • Submit: Click "Submit" to send your data. Our system will update the water quality index and map.

2. Report Water Pollution

  • Photo Upload: Attach a photo for better context and verification.
How to Use:

  • Select Location: Use the map to pinpoint the incident location or enter the address manually.
  • Submit Report: Provide necessary details and submit the report. The information will be added to our database and displayed on the water quality map.

4. Ground water Level Form
Purpose: Data Collection: Allow users to submit groundwater level data, including various parameters such as pre-monsoon and post-monsoon levels, fluctuation, and location details.
Data Visualization:Display groundwater data on an interactive map to visualize spatial variations and identify trends or issues related to water quality and levels.
Problem Analysis: Help in understanding the impact of groundwater fluctuation on different areas and assess the availability of water resources across Varanasi.
Features:
  • Map: Displays groundwater data using markers with color coding based on fluctuation levels (green, yellow, red).
    Each marker provides detailed information when clicked, including block, hydrograph station, and fluctuation levels.
  • Submission Form: Fields for entering block, hydrograph station, longitude, latitude, pre-monsoon level, post-monsoon level, and fluctuation. Form validation to ensure correct data entry.
  • Integration: Fetches existing data from a backend API and updates the map dynamically.
    Submits new data to the backend for storage and updates the map to reflect the latest entries.
  • Real-time Updates: Updates the map in real-time when new data is submitted, allowing users to see the most current information.
  • Data Coloring: Uses color coding to represent different levels of fluctuation, helping users quickly identify areas with significant issues.
How to Use:
  • View the Map: The map loads with existing groundwater data displayed as color-coded markers. You can zoom in and out or pan across the map to explore different areas.
  • Submit New Data: Fill out the data submission form on the left side of the screen. Enter information such as block, hydrograph station, longitude, latitude, pre-monsoon level, post-monsoon level, and fluctuation.
    Click the "Submit" button to send the data to the backend.
  • View Updated Map: After submission, the map automatically updates to include the newly added data. New markers are added based on the submitted information.
  • Analyze Data: Use the map’s visual cues to analyze groundwater levels and fluctuations across different blocks in Varanasi. Pay attention to the color coding for quick insights into areas with significant changes.
