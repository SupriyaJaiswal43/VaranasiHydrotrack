Water Quality Map

Displaying water quality data from various locations through map

Welcome to Varanasi HydroTrack, an interactive map application designed to provide comprehensive insights into the water quality of Varanasi. This tool aims to raise awareness about water quality issues, offer actionable insights, and support decision-making for local authorities, NGOs, and the general public. By visualizing various water quality parameters, Varanasi HydroTrack serves as a user-friendly platform for exploring and understanding the water quality data across the city.

Instructions

Map Overview
Explore water quality data in Varanasi by interacting with the map. Navigate through different areas to see water quality indicators.
Map Navigation
Use map controls to zoom in and out, and pan across the map. You can also scroll to zoom.
Marker Details
Click on a marker to view detailed water quality information for that specific location.
Heat Map Details
The heatmap layer shows the density of water quality measurements across different regions.
Marker Color Coding

Green: WQI ≤ 50 (Good)

Yellow: WQI 51-100 (Moderate)

Red: WQI > 100 (Poor)

Click on a marker for more detailed data and analysis.

Toggle Heat Map

Check to display the heat map layer showing measurement density.

Toggle Markers

Check to show/hide individual markers indicating Water Quality Index (WQI).

Toggle Clusters

Check to enable/disable clustering of nearby markers.

Toggle Satellite View

Check to switch to satellite view; uncheck for street view.

Location Dropdown

Select a location to zoom into that area.

Download Data

Click to download water quality data as a CSV file.

Color Coding Explanation

The chart uses color coding to help you easily interpret water quality data:

  • Red: Indicates that the measured value exceeds the recommended limit for that parameter. This suggests potential quality issues that need attention.
  • Blue: Indicates that the measured value is within the acceptable range. This signifies that the water quality for that parameter is generally good.
