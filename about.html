Project Overview

Varanasi HydroTrack is an innovative initiative designed to monitor and visualize the water quality of Varanasi. Utilizing modern technologies, our project aims to provide a comprehensive overview of water quality parameters, thereby contributing to environmental awareness and public health.

Water Quality Index (WQI)

The Water Quality Index (WQI) is a standardized metric used to evaluate the overall quality of surface waters. It is similar to the Trophic State Index (TSI), which is used for lakes and estuaries. Both indices serve as non-regulatory indicators that help identify water bodies needing further study. However, they are not used to determine compliance with water quality standards under the Impaired Waters Rule of the Clean Water Act. In Varanasi HydroTrack, WQI is used to assess streams, black waters (natural tea- and coffee-colored waters), and springs. The WQI values are calculated yearly by averaging the index values of selected water quality parameters. These parameters include pH, TDS, Turbidity, Iron(Fe), Calcium(Ca), Magnesium(Mg), Nitrate(NO3-), Fluoride(F-), Chloride(Cl-), Sulfate(SO4^2-,) Alkalinity, Hardness(as CaCO3).

Data Collection and Processing

Data for Varanasi HydroTrack is collected following established quality assurance rules to ensure accuracy and reliability. The data is collected from various research papers and field studies conducted over the years. The collected data is then processed and stored in CSV and JSON formats, which are subsequently uploaded to our MongoDB database for further analysis and visualization.

Functionality and Data Flow

Users can submit water quality data through a web form. The submitted data is validated and then stored in both CSV and JSON formats. This data is uploaded to the MongoDB database, ensuring that the most recent information is available for visualization.
Once the data is stored, it is processed to calculate the Water Quality Index (WQI). The processed data is visualized using Chart.js, providing clear and interactive charts that display water quality trends over time. Users can customize the charts based on selected time periods and other parameters, enabling a detailed analysis of water quality.

Project Goals

The primary goal of Varanasi HydroTrack is to provide a reliable and accessible platform for monitoring water quality in Varanasi. By making this information available to the public, we aim to raise awareness about water pollution and its impacts on health and the environment. Additionally, the platform can serve as a valuable tool for researchers and policymakers to identify areas needing attention and to implement effective water management strategies.

Conclusion

Varanasi HydroTrack is a step towards a sustainable and informed approach to water quality management in Varanasi. By leveraging modern technologies and data-driven insights, we aim to contribute to the well-being of the community and the preservation of our natural resources.
